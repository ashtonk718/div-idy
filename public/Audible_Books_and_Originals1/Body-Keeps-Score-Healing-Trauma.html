The Body Keeps the Score: Brain, Mind, and Body in the Healing of Trauma

  • Number one New York Times best seller“Essential reading for anyone interested in understanding and treating traumatic stress and the scope of its impact on society.” (Alexander McFarlane, director of the Centre for Traumatic Stress Studies)A pioneering researcher transforms our understanding of trauma and offers a bold new paradigm for healing in this New York Times best seller.Trauma is a fact of life. Veterans and their families deal with the painful aftermath of combat; one in five Americans has been molested; one in four grew up with alcoholics; one in three couples have engaged in physical violence. Dr. Bessel van der Kolk, one of the world’s foremost experts on trauma, has spent more than three decades working with survivors. In The Body Keeps the Score, he uses recent scientific advances to show how trauma literally reshapes both body and brain, compromising sufferers’ capacities for pleasure, engagement, self-control, and trust. He explores innovative treatments - from neurofeedback and meditation to sports, drama, and yoga - that offer new paths to recovery by activating the brain’s natural neuroplasticity. Based on Dr. van der Kolk’s own research and that of other leading specialists, The Body Keeps the Score exposes the tremendous power of our relationships both to hurt and to heal - and offers new hope for reclaiming lives.Cover image: 2020 Succession H. Pros & Cons

Pros Cons
Provides a deep understanding of trauma and its impact on individuals. Detailed traumatic experiences discussed in the book can be triggering for some readers.
Offers hope, validation, and scientific explanations for individuals dealing with trauma. Some readers may find certain parts overly detailed or difficult to read due to personal experiences.
Encourages self-empowerment and provides practical tools for healing. May require additional support or therapy alongside the book's content for some individuals.
Addresses different types of trauma and emphasizes the importance of physical movement in healing. Complex terminology and in-depth discussions may be challenging for casual readers.
Written with empathy, care, and humanity towards trauma survivors. Limited to a specific audience of individuals dealing with trauma-related issues.
Combines clinical cases, neuroscience, and powerful tools for a comprehensive perspective on trauma. Some readers may not fully resonate with the author's approach or therapeutic methods.

Div-idy's Recommendation

"The Body Keeps the Score" by Dr. Bessel van der Kolk is a profound exploration of trauma and its effects on individuals, offering deep insights, validation, and hope for those dealing with past experiences. Despite potentially triggering content and complex discussions, the book stands as a valuable resource for understanding trauma and providing pathways to healing. Readers seeking a holistic view of trauma, practical tools for recovery, and a compassionate approach to addressing mental health challenges will find this book highly beneficial. It's a recommended read for individuals interested in exploring the complexities of trauma and its impact on personal well-being."
