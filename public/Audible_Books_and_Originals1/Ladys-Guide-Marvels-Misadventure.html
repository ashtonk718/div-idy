A Lady's Guide to Marvels and Misadventure

  • Miss Clara Marie Stanton's family may be eccentric, but they certainly aren't insane. London, England, 1860: When Clara's ex-fiancé begins to spread rumors that her family suffers from hereditary insanity, it's all she can do to protect them from his desperate schemes, society's prejudice, and a lifetime in an asylum.
  • Then Clara's grandfather Drosselmeyer brings on an apprentice with a mechanical leg, and all pretense of normalcy takes wing.
  • Theodore Kingsley, a shame-chased vagabond haunted by the war, wants a fresh start far from Kingsley Court and the disappointed father who declared him dead. Upon returning to England, Theodore meets clockmaker Drosselmeyer, who hires him as an apprentice, much to Clara's dismay.
  • When Drosselmeyer spontaneously disappears in his secret flying owl machine, he leaves behind a note for Clara, beseeching her to make her dreams of adventure a reality by joining him on a merry scavenger hunt across Europe.
  • Together, Clara and Theodore set off to follow Drosselmeyer's trail of clues, but they will have to stay one step ahead of a villain who wants the flying machine for himself—at any cost.

Pros & Cons

Pros Cons
Rich and enchanting storytelling with a mix of adventure, romance, and whimsy. Some readers may find the religious themes to be too pervasive or distraction from the story.
Well-developed characters with multifaceted personalities and realistic struggles. Occasional predictability in plot development and character arcs.
Engaging and thoughtful exploration of themes like self-sufficiency, worthiness, and surrender. Some readers may find certain scenes depicting past trauma and abuse to be distressing.
Incorporation of classic literary references and steampunk elements for added charm and depth. Potential need for caution for readers sensitive to heavy religious content or family conflict themes.
Beautifully descriptive settings that immerse readers in a whimsical and fantastical world. May require suspension of disbelief for some fantastical elements introduced in the story.

Div-idy's Recommendation

Angela Bell's debut novel, "A Lady's Guide to Marvels and Misadventure," offers a rich and enchanting storytelling experience filled with adventure, romance, and whimsy. The well-developed characters, engaging thematic exploration, and incorporation of classic literary references make it a compelling read for fans of historical fiction and steampunk. While some readers may find the religious themes pervasive and certain scenes distressing, the overall depth and charm of the narrative make it a worthwhile journey. If you enjoy immersive storytelling with thoughtful themes and vibrant characters, this book is a recommended addition to your reading list.
