House of Flame and Shadow: Crescent City, Book 3
  • The stunning third book in the sexy, action-packed Crescent City series, following the global best sellers House of Earth and Blood and House of Sky and Breath. Bryce Quinlan never expected to see a world other than Midgard, but now that she has, all she wants is to get back. Everything she loves is in Midgard: her family, her friends, her mate. Stranded in a strange new world, she's going to need all her wits about her to get home again. And that's no easy feat when she has no idea whom to trust. Hunt Athalar has found himself in some deep holes in his life, but this one might be the deepest of all. After a few brief months with everything he ever wanted, he's in the Asteri's dungeons again, stripped of his freedom and without a clue as to Bryce's fate. He's desperate to help her, but until he can escape the Asteri's leash, his hands are quite literally tied. Pros & Cons

Pros Cons
High quality writing with engaging plot. Some parts felt slow and overly detailed leading to potential missed critical points.
Interesting character arcs and interactions. Crossover with ACOTAR series felt heavy and somewhat disappointing for some readers.
Surprising plot twists and emotional moments. Occasional instances of plot armor may reduce tension and impact of events.
Well-developed world-building and immersive storytelling. Desire for more depth in certain character storylines and interactions.
Evolving relationships between characters. Questions and uncertainties left for future books that may disappoint some readers.
Intriguing mysteries and potential for further exploration in the series. Perceived rushed conclusion to certain story elements.

Div-idy's Recommendation

The Crescent City series continues to deliver an engaging narrative with its unique characters, intricate world-building, and surprising plot developments. Readers are drawn into a realm filled with mysteries, emotional connections, and unexpected twists. Although some pacing issues and heavy crossover elements may slightly detract from the experience, the series overall offers a rich tapestry of storytelling. For fans of Sarah J. Maas' work and those seeking an immersive fantasy experience, Crescent City is a highly recommended journey worth embarking on.
