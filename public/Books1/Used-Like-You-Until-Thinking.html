I Used to Like You Until...: (How Binary Thinking Divides Us)
  • In this whip-smart follow-up to the “ruthlessly honest exploration of comedy and cancellation” (Dr. Drew) You Can’t Joke About That, New York Times bestselling author Kat Timpf examines the dangers of binary thinking, and how it threatens to take over our institutions, relationships, and even our freedoms.
  • For some reason, when it comes to complex issues, we’ve largely limited ourselves to just two options, resulting in a society of non-thinkers. After all, once you’ve picked a side, all the thinking has already been done for you.
  • Binary thinking is much more than just the enemy of critical thinking, it’s also an immediate danger to our political discourse, our institutions, our way of consuming news, our relationships, our creativity, and even to our freedoms.
  • All too often, we will let a single difference in viewpoint, an assumption, or an association be enough to write off another person entirely, even if we know nothing else about them.
  • Through humorous examples from her own life and insight, only someone in her bizarre position can possess, Kat reminds us that the world doesn’t have to be so black and white.
  • In her signature witty voice, Kat inspires us to lean into thoughtful consideration, genuine conversation, vulnerability, and only hating people when they really deserve it.

Div-idy's Recommendation

The book offers a mix of insightful personal anecdotes and deep reflections on societal matters, catering to readers seeking thought-provoking content. Despite some divisive viewpoints and a preference for personal stories over cited studies, the book succeeds in sparking discussions and encouraging critical thinking. Readers looking for a balanced and engaging read with a focus on individual experiences will likely find value in this book. It's recommended for those open to diverse perspectives and willing to engage with challenging topics.
