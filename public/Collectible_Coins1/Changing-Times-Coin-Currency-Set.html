Changing Times Coin & Currency Set
  • Modern coins and banknotes have changed dramatically over the last half century, bringing back memories of bygone days. This collection features several items that were once commonly found in circulation but are now increasingly hard to find and widely sought-after for their timeless designs and compelling stories.
  • America’s beloved Lincoln Wheatback Penny, last minted in 1958, features two stalks of wheat on the back and a portrait of Lincoln on the obverse that is still used today.
  • The original Jefferson Nickel from 1938, honoring Thomas Jefferson, was last made in 2003 with an obverse depicting a portrait of Jefferson created by French sculptor Jean-Antoine Houdon in 1789.
  • The Roosevelt Dime, first minted in 1946, was produced in 90% silver until 1964. It honors President Franklin D. Roosevelt and is included in this collection as a 90% silver coin.
  • The original Washington Quarter was issued in 1932 to celebrate George Washington’s 200th birthday, featuring a portrait by John Flanagan that is still used today. The reverse design has changed to State Quarters and National Parks Quarters since 1999.
  • The $1 Silver Certificate, last made in the Series of 1957, could be redeemed for actual silver until June 24, 1968, with a blue Treasury seal distinguishing it from current paper currency.
  • This collection is presented in a custom coin wallet with a narrative detailing the history and design of each item.
